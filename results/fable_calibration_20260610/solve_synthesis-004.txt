A hospital system is evaluating three architectures for their new electronic health records (EHR) platform that must serve 12 hospitals, 4,000 physicians, and meet HIPAA requirements:

(a) Monolithic application with a single shared database
(b) Microservices with per-service databases and an API gateway
(c) Modular monolith with domain boundaries enforced by module interfaces

For each architecture, analyse:
- Data consistency and cross-domain queries (e.g. 'show all medications for patient X across all departments')
- Compliance and audit trail requirements under HIPAA
- Deployment and rollback risk across 12 hospitals
- Team scaling (currently 40 developers, growing to 120 over 2 years)
- Failure blast radius and availability

Your analysis must address a specific technical tension: the platform needs both real-time cross-department patient views AND the ability for individual hospitals to customise workflows without affecting others. Explain how each architecture handles (or fails to handle) this tension.

Conclude with a phased recommendation (what to build first, what to migrate to later) with explicit criteria for when to trigger each phase transition.
